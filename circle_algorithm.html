Interactive companion to the grokked mod-113 network. 2 · Dials and shadows (why rows are sinusoids)

The point walks the k-wound pegs. Each embedding dimension is a fixed axis (a “dial”) through the plane, with its own orientation φ and gain α. Its stored value is α × the point's shadow on that axis — and the shadow of circular motion is a sinusoid. The u and v axes are nothing special: just two convenient dials (φ=0 and φ=90°, α=1). Stack every dial's α·cosφ into one vector and its α·sinφ into another — those are the u/v basis vectors the DFT hands back, one entry per dimension. Any two non-parallel dials already pin the point exactly (tick “reconstruct” below: divide each stored value by its α, stand a perpendicular on each axis at that shadow, and the perpendiculars intersect at the point). So one frequency needs only 2 dimensions — which is why the readout's effective rank collapses to 2 × (#key frequencies): 128 dials are massively redundant coordinates for the same few circles.

3 · The DFT: freeze and stack, or spin and cancel

Signal: a real cosine at frequency ksig with phase φ — i.e. a mirror-pair of unit rotators (+k and −k). The probe counter-rotates everything at kprobe. Arrows are added tip-to-tail over all 113 tokens. If the probe matches, the +k rotator freezes: 113 parallel arrows stack into a straight wall of length 113/2, pointing at the phase angle. Everything else keeps spinning and its path closes into a rosette that returns to zero.

4 · Scoring by interference (why several frequencies)

logit(c) = Σk∈S cos(2πk(a+b−c)/113). At the correct c every frequency votes +1 simultaneously; elsewhere the cosines scatter and cancel. Each added frequency sharpens the peak and widens the margin — one k is a correct algorithm, five is a confident one. Each ticked k's own cosine is drawn faintly: the blue curve is literally their sum. And below the chart, the dot product behind one logit is spelled out in numbers: the readout carries (cos, sin) of the sum-point on every circle; candidate c's frozen unembedding row carries (cos, sin) of c's own peg on the same circles; logit(c) is their plain dot product — per circle, cos·cos + sin·sin = cos(angle difference). That is all the “spectrum” is: 113 dot products against 113 stock rows.

5 · The whole algorithm, end to end (no network)

The algorithm itself, in closed form — no waves, no scoring, no network. Pick a and b, tick some frequencies, press run. Embed: on each circle, x lands at angle 2πk·x/113 — peg (k·x) mod 113. Add: b's angle is laid onto the end of a's; passing a full turn just wraps — the mod costs nothing, the circle does it. Ungear: we built this machine, so we know k — the sum sits on peg s = k·(a+b) mod 113, and c = k−1·s mod 113, exactly (k−1 exists because 113 is prime). After a run, read the labels: every circle's red peg encodes the same answer c, while its immediate neighbor pegs encode numbers k−1 apart — same answer everywhere, completely different peg-to-number dictionaries. The trained network is handed none of this: it doesn't know k, and its linear readout can't take an arctan — all it can do is dot products. So it must score all 113 candidates and let interference pick the winner: that is Panel 4, and the real-model section below shows the network actually doing it. {5, 8, 17, 49} is the circuit the trained run chose.

6 · The two-dial machine (build your own — or curse it)

The middle rung of the ladder. Panel 5 is the pure algorithm (closed form, nothing learned); the real-model section below is the trained network (128 dimensions, redundancy, noise). This is the ideal tiny model: the same transformer machinery — lookup tables, dot products, argmax — hand-tuned to run the algorithm on minimum hardware: two embedding dimensions, one frequency, and phases you choose. We are manually implementing the algorithm in the architecture; training hopes to approximate this tuning. Every stage below is the machine's real data path. Hover any table cell: each entry is just α·cos(θx − φd) — one token's angle read through one dial, trig carved into weights, nothing dynamic. With cursed phases the dossiers trace an ellipse (junk amplitude E = |α₁²e2iφ₁ + α₂²e2iφ₂|/(α₁²+α₂²); axes √((α₁²+α₂²)(1±E)/2)) and the argmax walks away from the truth. Heal to a circle and watch 113/113. The combine stage is assumed perfect for now — attention (fetch) and the MLP (angle addition) get folded into this machine once Acts 2–3 are owned.

①b · the health identities — the doubled-phase arrows must close

Each arrow is one dial's term, tip-to-tail; the red dot is the sum, and health = the loop closes back to the origin. Circle condition, per plane: Σd αd²·e2iφd = 0. With two dials that is α₁²∠2φ₁ + α₂²∠2φ₂ = 0 — two arrows cancel only if they have equal lengths (α₁ = α₂: unequal gains stretch the circle into an ellipse along a dial axis, your “different means”) and exactly opposite directions (2φ₂ = 2φ₁ + π, i.e. φ₂ = φ₁ ± 90°: any other gap shears it). One complex equation, two real requirements: equal loudness, quarter-turn apart. Plane orthogonality, across notes: Σd zd,1·z̄d,2 = 0 and Σd zd,1·zd,2 = 0, where zd,f = αd,f·eiφd,f — the per-dial products of the two notes' weights, in difference-phase and sum-phase form. Dedicated dials satisfy both trivially (every product is 0); shared dials must cancel them by arrangement — and with only 2 dials, these plus each note's own circle condition have no common solution, which is why “collide” can't be healed.

②a · the attention stage — how “=” fetches a and b into one register

  softmax sharpness γ 6.0

The 3-dim toy: each token's residual is (dial 1, dial 2, type) — numbers carry type +1, “=” carries −1. W_Q reads “=”'s type and flips it to +γ; W_K strips all but type; the frozen “=” query then scores +γ on each operand, −γ on itself; softmax → (½, ½, ~0) as γ grows; W_V copies the plane and drops type, so the weighted average is ½·a + ½·b — the half-angle point, which walks out in “=”'s residual (with a vestigial −1 riding along). Only the highlighted “=” row is used; the a- and b-query rows write into positions the readout never looks at.

②b · the MLP — five ReLUs double the angle

This runs on the actual attention output in dial coordinates — so it feels your phases live. Five ReLU neurons at fixed axes 72° apart project the incoming point (→ cos(β−ψⱼ), β = its dial-angle), rectify (→ an octave cos(2(β−ψⱼ)) is born), and the output layer sums them so only the doubled term survives — pointing at 2β, then rotated by φ₁ so it lands in the frame the dossiers live in (the output weights are phase-locked to the embedding — the co-adaptation training finds; without it, doubling would double the frame's rotation and miss). With an orthonormal embedding (hit heal or tick ideal mode) the input is a true circle point and the output lands on colc. Curse the gains or perpendicularity and the input becomes an ellipse point these fixed neurons can't un-shear — it drifts off. Slide φ and α and watch.
